# Literature Review: The "Korea Discount"

This document summarizes the core academic and practitioner literature regarding the persistent undervaluation of South Korean equities.

## 1. Primary Drivers of the Discount
Literature identifies three structural "pillars" that contribute to the Korea Discount:

### A. Corporate Governance & Chaebol Structure
*   **Agency Costs & Tunneling:** Controlling families (Chaebols) often use complex cross-shareholding networks to exert control far exceeding their economic stake. Research by **Baek, Kang, & Park (2003)** highlights how this structure leads to "tunneling" (moving assets to benefit controlling families at the expense of minority shareholders).
*   **Double Counting:** The practice of listing both parent companies and multiple subsidiaries leads to "equity dilution" and valuation confusion in global markets.
*   **Chaebol-Specific Discount:** Empirical analysis (e.g., Diva-portal, 2025) suggests a ~26.8% discount specifically for Chaebol-affiliated firms after controlling for fundamentals.

### B. Shareholder Returns & Taxation
*   **Low Payout Ratios:** Historically, Korean firms have significantly lower dividend payout ratios compared to TOPIX or S&P 500 peers.
*   **Inheritance Tax Incentives:** South Korea’s high inheritance tax (up to 60%) creates a perverse incentive for controlling families to keep share prices depressed to minimize tax liabilities during generational transfers.

### C. Geopolitical Risk (GPR)
*   **The "North Korea Premium":** While often cited, recent studies (e.g., **IMF Working Paper 21/251**) suggest that while North Korean provocations increase systematic risk (Beta), they may not be the *primary* driver of the structural discount, which is increasingly attributed to governance.

## 2. Policy Reforms & The "Japan Parallel"
*   **Natural Experiment:** Japan’s 2023–2024 corporate governance reforms (led by the Tokyo Stock Exchange) serve as a blueprint. The "comply or explain" model for firms trading below 1.0x P/B is a focal point for current Korean "Value-Up" policy analysis.
*   **Value-Up Program (2024/2025):** The Korean government's recent shift from voluntary to more mandatory disclosures (e.g., Commercial Act amendments) aims to emulate the "Japanese Miracle."

## 3. Market Accessibility
*   Deficiencies in foreign exchange hours and English-language disclosures have historically limited international institutional inflows, a key requirement for MSCI Developed Market reclassification.
